About Me

Hello, I'm Nishant, a postdoctoral fellow in mathematics, currently working in the Department of Mathematical Sciences at IISER Mohali, India, under the mentorship of Prof. Mahender Singh. My research focuses on the algebraic structures arising from set-theoretical solutions of the Yang-Baxter equation. Key concepts in my work include Rota-Baxter operators and skew braces, as well as their broader implications in mathematics.

Research Interests

My research interests lie in the study of set-theoretical solutions of the Yang-Baxter equation. Additionally, I work on Rota-Baxter operators, skew left braces, and their extensions, including low-dimensional cohomology for relative Rota-Baxter groups. I am also exploring the relationship between the Yang-Baxter equation and knot theory, braid theory, and quantum groups.
